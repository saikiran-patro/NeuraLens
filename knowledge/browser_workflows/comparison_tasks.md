# Comparison Tasks

## Build a stable rubric

Choose three to six criteria before collecting options. Give each criterion a clear measurement and mark whether it is required, preferred, or informational.

## Normalize evidence

Compare the same units, date ranges, plan tiers, and included features. Separate facts from estimates and record the source or page section for important claims.

## Expose tradeoffs

Do not collapse everything into one score too early. Present the best fit for each priority, then explain the compromise. Missing information should remain visibly unknown.

## Confirm current context

Web content and availability change. Recheck decisive details on the final page before the user commits time, money, or personal data.
